The truck is “mocked-up” for these photos. The front tire is simply leaning against the inside fender. There are several issues I’m working on that continue to delay the final production. This project is the most involved that we’ve tackled here at SAMS and we want to do it right, so bear with us and we will all arrive together.
No details are in place such as door hinges, mirror, door handle etc.
Front view looks kind’a derelict, though its really just areas where I’ve been refining the shape of the fenders. There is no tire on the passenger side in this shot.  

The proportions of the truck look good with our “Sanders” figure. The truck is 16’ long, 6’3” high and 5’3” wide. The bed is just over 6’ long and is 4’ wide.

There are tailgate and tail light castings. The ivory colored parts are castings while the Grey and dark parts are original patterns.  

Here is how the truck looks along side an Accucraft T boiler Shay. I think it looks about right, the Shay is a small loco and the truck does not overwhelm it even photographed in front. It does not look too small in comparison, either.
