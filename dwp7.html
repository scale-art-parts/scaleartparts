The photo above overlooks the town of Virginia, MN and the West Virginia yard; note the roundhouse upper left. The big open area in the center of the photo is an operator’s pit for the movement of trains in and around Virginia. The DWP gained access to Virginia by means of a loop line that diverted from the mainline which continued to the yard. An ornate depot served the DWP in Virginia on the edge of Silver Lake. The depot still exists, but is now a bank. There is also a large coal fired power plant located next to the depot. In the foreground is a compressed  version of Rainy Junction where the DWP interchanged with the DMIR. Virginia will be one of the busiest areas on the layout with lots of switching. In the background is the unfinished double-sided wrap around backdrop/scene divider. The layout has several two-sided backdrops.
Finally, what we see too little of.....a train running on the DWP! This is a Weaver 2-8-0 diecast locomotive. It is a capable little locomotive that has pulled 25 freight cars around the layout. The locos need some modification for power pick-up as they tend to stall on the Atlas turnouts, (dead frogs). We have added power pick-up to both tender trucks and added wipers to the fireman’s side of the loco. We are using MRC large scale sound decoders. The conversion is not difficult. There will be many more action shots soon. We are very nearly done with the construction phase and scenery, buildings and details will begin making this O scale railroad come alive with the essence of 1950’s Minnesota. Stay tuned, more updates are on the way!
We call this the “Laurentian Divider”. Here the track curves back after curving out of West Virginia yard and climbs up and over the Laurentian Divide. This divider splits the loop into two distinct scenes: a gravel pit on one side and pulp wood loading on the other.
The other side of the “Laurentian Divider”. The mainline curves around on the right side of the photo and continues on till it again curves to the right and heads out across the long elevated stretch above Duluth. It will cross the Pratt truss bridge over the entrance and eventually show up on the mainline in the left of the photo which is the town of Ranier, MN. The duck-under at the far end of this view is the only duck-under a mainline operator must negotiate. It is, unfortunately, long with little headroom. A rolling trolley will be installed to help with the movement through this “tunnel”.  
A little more panoramic look at Virginia. To the right in the photo the imposing Rouchleau ore crusher will be located. This structure crushed the iron ore brought in from the Oliver Mining Company holdings which was then transported the the Lake Superior Ore Docks in Duluth by the Duluth, Missabe & Iron Range RR.  The 2 story depot is temporarily displaced from its West Duluth home. Note the long run of two-sided backdrop. The area occupied by the tool carts will be another operator’s pit for switching the Rouchleau facility. Access is through one of three duck-unders.
To the left this photo shows the Rouchleau duck-under as it is being enclosed with hardboard. Where possible the overhead track and supporting scenery is kept narrow to help minimize head-banging. This location can be navigated while squatting.
The photo to the right is of the duck-under for the Virginia operator’s pit. Here again the right of way over the duck-under is narrow. Hand grabs will be installed to aid movement through these duck-unders. Mainline operation does not require ducking under in these two locations.
