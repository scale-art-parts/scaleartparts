The Robert. E. Lee Mine

The R.E. Lee mine sat on Fryer Hill not far from the famous Matchless Mine of Baby Doe Tabor fame. This model depicts the mine in the period of the late 1870’s when it hit peak production of silver.

The model is built to 1/24 th scale or 1/2”=1’. It is entirely scratch-built from photos and by researching standard practices of the day. The figures are an assortment of commercial large scale folks. The hoist operates and the cage moves up and down from the machinery floor to the bottom of the shaft. The underground is illuminated with tiny “candles” stuck into the rock walls. The timbering is a proper square set in miniature.

If you find yourself in Leadville, CO be sure to visit the National Mining Hall of Fame and Museum on 9th street.

This is the set up that makes the cage go up and down. The cable runs on the big acrylic disc which is power by a Hankscraft motor. The direction is reversed by a Circuitron autoreverse circuit board. The light bulb is for consistent lighting for the optic sensors that “tell” the mechanism when to reverse. There is a counter weight visible in the background. The thing works pretty reliably day in and day out. The motion is activated by a button on the front of the exhibit which applies power through a 3 minute timer.
