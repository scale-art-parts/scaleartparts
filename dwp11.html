The area around the West Duluth roundhouse is beginning to take shape. A Weaver 2-8-0 pulls a short freight  on the mainline past the yard which is nearly finished getting ballasted. A couple of die-cast vehicles are trying out the new dirt road that still is in need of a crossing. Most of the ground foam is from Timberline Scenery Products.

John Crawford reconfigured the hillside Southeast of the West Duluth yard. In truth this hill does not exist in this area, but we were compelled to put a hill to support the upper level track that crosses over . Even though it is not true to prototype, we all think it creates a great scene and provides us with a view block.  

Looking over the turntable into the doors of the West Duluth roundhouse affords a view of some of the details that have been placed inside.

Here is Jeff McDonald plugging away at ballasting the West Duluth yard. We are using cinder ballast from Timberline Scenery Products. It is a crushed walnut shell ballast which is lightweight  and can be rather finicky to use until you get the hang of it. We find that the ballast must be thoroughly saturated with wet water prior to using the glue mix. The water-glue & detergent mix must be applied carefully to avoid puddles or some ballast will float. We use a pump type garden sprayer to apply the wet water and empty glue bottles for the glue mix. Our ballast glue mix is about 3 parts water to 1 part white glue with several drops of dish detergent added per ½ gallon of mix.

Dry ballast is poured over the track and spread with a soft paint brush. In the yard; the entire area is covered with the cinder ballast as well as the track.

The West Duluth yard looking toward the roundhouse. It required several days to ballast all of this area.

The Plasticville trailer park is beginning to look well established as the backdrop has been painted and some basic scenery has begun to take shape. Looks like the woman could not keep her footing in the excitement of the landscaping going on around her! The fellow with the beer seems to take it all in stride. These figures are from Woodland Scenics.

A lot of the hardshell has been painted an earth color and some basic ground foam texturing has been done in preparation for more detailed scenery yet to come.

The over/under leading into the mountain loop looks much better with a bit of paint and ground cover. Some of the unpainted hardshell can be seen in the background.
