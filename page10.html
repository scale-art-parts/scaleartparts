Scale Art model Services is primarily, Scott Anderson.  Scott has been building custom railroad models for over 25 years for a great variety of clients. Most of my work has centered around the Rocky Mountain Region. A few projects have taken on a more “national “ scope as I have completed projects in Illinois, Texas, Oregon and a new project recently started in the Mid-West . I work in all scales and enjoy the challenges each scale has to offer.   Scott is accomplished in most every aspect of the model railroad hobby and enjoys new challenges and the opportunity to learn new techniques.  SAMS builds complete layouts from design to completion or any phase in between. The creation of super realistic scenery has long been the hallmark of Scott’s work. Scott’s participation in the building of Tom Miller’s fabulous F scale Narrow Gauge Railway was an opportunity to hone his skills in designing and building numerous buildings from scratch. Although Scott take a personal interest in all projects; we also have an available group of highly talented modelers to call in when the need requires additional hands or individuals with unique talents to contribute. Each team member has his or her particular areas of expertise which allow us to tackle most any project with confidence. We can do Garden Railway installations or even N scale coffee table layouts. We have installed some terrific overhead railway systems for both private clients as well as Children’s Hospital in Denver. We are certain we can provide you with the skills and creativity that will set your project apart from the everyday.

Scott scrutinizes the installation of his  fully animated 1:20.3, (Fn3), stamp mill built for Tom Miller in Portland, Oregon. Scott worked for three years scratch-building a variety of structures; large and small for this amazing large scale layout. Scott is well known in the Denver area and beyond for his uncanny ability to capture the follies and foibles of the human condition in miniature. The hallmark of Scott’s work is subtle humor that makes viewing his work just that much more fun. Scott has presented clinics on topics such as Weathering, Building and finishing craftsman structure kits, and Scenery. You will find Scott’s work featured as displays in Caboose Hobbies of Denver and the National Mining Hall of Fame and Museum in Leadville, Colorado as well as many private homes and collections.  

About Scale Art Models

Joe Crea, well known Narrow Gauge and Shortline Gazette author, collaborated closely with Scott on several areas of Tom Miller’s Fn3 layout. Joe’s sense of time, place and ability to capture the “Human element” resulted in some of the most arresting scenes on Tom’s layout as well as some of the most whimsical.

Alan Olson is a frequent contributor to Garden Railways Magazine. He has been working on a “SAMS” outdoor railway project for the past two years. The client has enjoyed the railway so much that it continues to grow bigger each year! Alan has a remarkable ability with large scale trains and locomotives and is well known for his conversions of off the shelf equipment.
