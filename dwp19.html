Because of the complexity of this structure build and since I kind of left the build hanging in the air, I am devoting another entry, (at least) to the DWP depot at Virginia, Minnesota. I alluded on page 18 that we are now using a laser cutter. We bought a small desktop machine in December 2012 to aid in these custom structure builds. As I am dedicated to getting the appearance of building like this depot as close to prototype as possible, things like windows and doors can be a real slog if you build them up by hand. The curved top windows in the second story would have been a real pain to fabricate. The laser made all this super easy for us. We are continually finding more ways we can use it. As time permits the doors and windows that you see in the custom built structures will be offered as parts to you scratch-builders out there. Watch for them.

In this photo the sub-roof card is being test fit. The etched in lines for the row of shingles in easy to see here. With the addition of the roofs the depot becomes quite imposing in size. The roof overhang is about 8 scale feet. 12 pieces make up the main, lower level roof. There are simple brackets that fit under the roof which you will see further along.

This shot shows several things; the paper figure in the dispatcher’s office on the upper level, the complexity of the roof edge where it wraps around the upper story window and some of the detail that has been added such as window shades. Interior lighted is installed and a degree of interior detail is also included.

In this overall shot much is now completed. The shingle, however, are only halfway to completion. All of the stone trim and corner trim is in place as is all of the wood trim except around the base of the peaked roof. The shingles are laser cut strips. Some are visible to the lower left. They are cut from textured charcoal paper with an adhesive peel and stick backing. Even so, it takes a great deal of time to apply the strips and get them lined up properly. So far there are 200 10” long strips on the completed roofs.  We figure another 200 to finish. There is still a lot of touch up painting to do as well as weathering. The platform has been started but is not yet ready for the depot to sit on it.

This is partly why the shingles take so long. There is a lot of cutting in and around, but it sure looks nice. This is the upper story window again. It was know as the Crow’s Nest and was home to the dispatcher. He had a swell lake view and the towers gave him a pretty fair view of the tracks coming and going. The ornate sandstone ornament above the window is speculative. We could not really make it out, but it sure looks like a shock of wheat, so that is what we made it to be on the model.

A down low photo shows the roof brackets and roof edge trim. There is a peek inside the open freight door showing some of the freight waiting to be moved. The curled up paper on this end is from the adhesive backing. Not much longer and the depot will be moved to it’s home on the DWP layout. The trestle is on it’s way out to be installed later this week along with a number of other pieces.
