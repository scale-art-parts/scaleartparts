There are many talented model builders working away on numerous wonderful projects that will be seen by relative few individuals. Scale Art Parts is interested in seeing these models and sharing them with fellow enthusiasts. We are also interested to see how our products are finished and put to use by our customers. This Gallery is dedicated to you; the basement/backyard artists of the model railroad hobby. Enjoy, be inspired and send us your photos of your own masterpieces.

Bill Schoch of Pennsylvania has built some highly imaginative and wonderfully detailed structures for his 1:20.3 outdoor railway. The majority of the structures, with the exception of Slick’s appear to be built from Tom Yorke’s “Frijole Flats” plan book. A quick Google search confirms the similarity of the architecture and coloring. Tom also asserts that the “Critter” is from a kit he produced. Regardless of parentage, Bill has done an expert job interpreting these elements and bringing them to life in 1:20.3 scale.  Hats off to Bill for his fine craftsmanship and also to Tom Yorke for providing model railroaders with many years of inspiration and kits.

Jim McDaniel sent along a number of photos of his scratch-built models. Jim builds in 1:20.3 standard and narrow gauges. The outstanding passenger truck is one of two destined for a model of a 1901 Pullman private business car.

Mike Kerrigan sent us some photos of his modeling project; a diner that is overseen by “Ken the Cook”. Ken was painted by Mark and is now permanently in residence in the restaurant.  Mark informs that the various interior furnishings are from a variety of sources such as PlayMobile and dollhouse products.

David Silverton scratch-built this terrific model of the D&RGW Cumbres Depot in F scale. David used Scale Art Parts for the freight door, chimneys and passenger doors. The windows are from Grandt Line. The depot looks great in its outdoor setting. Thanks for sharing David.

Models & photos: David Silverton

Bob Santos is a recent parts customer who models his railroad subjects in 1/2” scale. Although our parts are mastered to 1:20.3 we have a number of individuals who model in 1/24 and “G” who find many of our parts suitable for their scale. Bob is a scratch-builder whose models are second to none. You can see more of Bob’s excellent models at: http://www.freewebs.com/santmod/
