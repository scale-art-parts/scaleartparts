We apologize for the lengthy pause in updating the progress on the DWP. Frankly it has not been real exciting stuff. Much more of the same things over and over again such as hard shell and backdrop painting. We have done some track changes and added some sidings for industries around Duluth as well as a newly configured Rainy junction and trackage around the iron ore crusher. All this required more wiring and track painting. It is fair to say that we are done with laying track. Done, done, done.....maybe. We took off a month in September so Scott could have a cancerous prostate removed. The surgery went well and all is good. We will be back on site in early October.

In the meantime...here are a few photos of some of the work we have been doing through the summer months.

This is a foamcore mock-up of the Rouchleau ore crusher near Virginia, Mn. It was originally used to crush iron ore which was shipped out on the Duluth, Missabe and Iron Range. In the mid 50’s it was converted to a Taconite pellet plant, which is our ultimate goal for this scene. Switchers from the Oliver mining company shoved loaded ore cars up the 4% grade to the top of the crusher. Missabe ore jennies loaded iron ore out the bottom of the plant bound for the ore docks at Duluth. In our scene we have a 45’ 4% approach to the upper level of the crusher. The loading track will continue through the crusher and out the opening facing us in the photo. The track is carried on a long steel trestle which is laying in the foreground. This area will be a great switching assignment , very challenging, but lots of fun.

Another view of Rainy Junction. Here you can see one of the panels. The hill for the grade is a bit compressed and looks taller and steeper than the prototype. Even on this layout some things have to be compromised to get them to fit. The climb to the crusher is 16 inches vertical from ground level.

Here we are looking across a very abbreviated Rainy Junction yard. This was an interchange point for the Missabe and the DWP. You can see that we had to create an operator’s pit between the yard and the crusher to allow access for switching the two areas. Most of the turnouts on the layout are hand throws, but here a number of them are out of reach and tortoise slow motion machines have been used. Those machines are thrown from three different panels, some of which duplicate the switches thrown from another panel. We are using blue foam board to fill in areas that will be flat. There will be a low hill in the center of the photo where the scenery is being formed.

Here is something we have been doing a lot of......basic ground cover. This gives us a base to build the rest of the scenery textures and colors on. It also helps to give the hardshell some resemblance to being finished.

This is the site of the paper mill we have planned. Being inside of a reverse loop confines the space a little so some of the mill will be on the backdrop. As at Rainy junction, we have a pit here also for access. All the turnout are reachable, however so no panels or Tortoise machines are needed.

We decided to use a modified Bachmann coal tower in the yard at West Virginia. It needed a coal dump so one was built in place out of scrap wood and styrene. In the photo at right you can see it under the coaling plant.  The silver tower is a sanding tower. In the background you can make out the turntable control build into the shed.
