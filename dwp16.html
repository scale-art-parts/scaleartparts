May was a productive month on the DWP. Most of the ground was completed in one aisle and numerous details were added to scenes in progress such as the trailer park and the hill. “Water” was modeled in Rock Creek just outside the West Duluth yard. This is the first water feature to be modeled on the layout, but it will not be the last. More details were added to the fallen barn scene and the highway through that scene has been worked on with color and details.

Enclosed water tanks were found along the DWP right of way. It was decided to model the tanks after the octagonal style  used by the Canadian National Railroad. This particular model is made from Scale Art Models own resin castings and is an impressive structure.  The pumphouse is from an unknown source. To create the site, first the hardshell was cut away and a base of Styrofoam was leveled and hot glued into place. The area was then blended in with plaster cloth and Sculptamold, (see photo below). An access road was modeled alongside the tracks as the scene was reworked. The blue tape is an effort to keep the mess off the painted and ballasted mainline.  

Looking at the scene from the same location as above shows what a difference a little ground cover can make. The two structures look right at home and open for business.     

For rail fans the trailer park is the perfect place to live as CN GP9 # 4307 moves a cut of freight cars in the West Duluth yard.  “Bliss Haven” may be wishful thinking for the trailer pack residents who don’t appreciate the noise, smoke and activity of a busy rail yard.

This little creek represents Kingsbury creek just West of the West Duluth yard. Part of the creek is painted on the backdrop while the remainder is modeled with acrylic paint and Gloss Medium. The gloss medium is built up in multiple layers to give the water depth. Blues and greens were painted in around the rocks before the gloss medium was applied. Each layer is allowed to fully cure or dry before the next layer is brushed on. The white water is Gloss gel teased up and allowed to dry than dry brushed with white acrylic paint.  

Once the colors are satisfactory ground cover can be added. This step really make the previous work “pop”.

This over view shows a variety of colors and textures in the ground cover. Most of the ground foam is from Timberline Scenery.

Pleas excuse the fuzziness of the photos. While the phone camera is pretty good, it lacks depth of field. Two final shots of the finished rock and surrounding scenery. There are still scores of trees to add to this area so there will be more photos to come.

Bliss Haven Estates is a scene that has gradually grown more detailed and more fun to view. A large sign was added along with some more trees, people, flowers and the nifty guard bear at the entrance to the park. The bear sign is a composite of web images combined in Photoshop.

It was decided that a house should be added down the road from Bliss Haven to give the area more people presence and to give the road more purpose. The chosen site was hilly and uneven, so it was necessary to excavate the site and install a level base. This was accomplished with Styrofoam, plaster cloth and Sculptamold just like the water tank site was created.

Once the plaster was dry the site was painted with our standard earth color which is a muddy brown flat latex paint and turf and grass blend ground foam was sprinkled into the the wet paint. Additional layers of various textures of ground foam were added along with real dirt. Finally some trees were planted around the house for some vertical detail. The lower photo shows the end result. The scene can still benefit from added details such as clutter, people, some small out buildings and more trees. As it is a background scene, it looks pretty good as is.
