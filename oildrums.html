Some 55 gallon oil drums

A customer sent me some photos of a group of beat up rusty 55 gallon drums. He wanted to know if I would paint and weather some of our drums for him. Of course we will do that! We can do it for you, too.

This group represents the drums that are in better condition than the rest. Once the castings were cleaned up, washed and primed with automotive Gray primer, they were given a spray coat of ruddy brown or oxide red. This was allowed to dry then Bragdon rust color powders were applied then sealed with clear. The blue and wine color drums were then painted their final colors; the orange drum was treated differantly...... .Read on below.

To achieve the nasty rusted look of the more battered drums they were prepared as above, but before painting the final color the drums had a coat of a craft store product called “Weathered Wood” brushed on. This is a clear gel-like medium that creates a resist to further color coats and causes it to crackle or alligator. Once the weathered wood dried, several various shades of orange were airbrushed onto the drums and allowed to dry for only a few minutes to half an hour. Using a stiff brush and plain water the finish on the drums was attacked, the weathered wood being soluble allowed the rusty undercoat to show through. The final steps are to work the rusty areas with a little more rust powder, some dry brushing and a final clear coat to seal everything up.

This was a fun project. Weathering is a creative endeavor that gives a model that realistic touch. We always look forward to doing projects like this for our customers so we hope you will consider some weathered drums for your railroad. We used 1 set of FA16-55 gallon drums and one set of FA26-Damaged drum. We substituted one upright damaged drum for the laying down drum that is normally included in FA26.
