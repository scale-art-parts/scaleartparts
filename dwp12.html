The two bridge areas on the mountain loop look much better with some scenery covering the hardshell.

Koki Creek is the top photo and Stewart Creek is the lower. The two bents have been remover from under the Stewart Creek bridge temporarily while scenery is underway.

While Simar Minnesota did not have a passenger depot, there was an operator’s station from which train orders were passed. Simar was also the turn around for helpers after the climb out of Duluth. There was a wye and we have that modeled here as well. The paint and ground cover have yet to make it this far, but the hardshell is complete.

Across the aisle from West Duluth. The MTH depot is standing in for the scratch-built Canadian National style depot yet to come.

This looks almost like a Christmas scene, but the snow is really just raw hardshell. This scene is on the end of an aisle and will be view when first entering the building. Here some of the key elements are checked for arrangement.

Here is a closer look at the area in the previous photo. The collapsed barn is a Laser Art kit and the inclusion of this structure is inspired by an actual barn we’ve seen along the highway on our travels.  The house as a Weaver product and the store is from MTH.

Ahhh, this is what we like to see. The Weaver 2-8-0 makes it way through the currently unprotected crossing. We have a set of Tomar crossing signals to place here. We have been experimenting with some fairly new electronic circuits to make the signals flash and bells sound. More detail on that to follow.
