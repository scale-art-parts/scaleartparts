Scale Art Model Services is first and foremost a custom builder service. Our parts line came about as specific parts or, as in the case of the REA truck; a particular model was needed to complete requests by clients. The REA truck model is a neat piece and certainly prototypically ubiquitous from the 20’s through the 70’s, but most common in the form modeled by us in the 40’s and 50’s. The cowl length delivery truck was seen everywhere throughout the nation until it was replaced with the increasingly common step van in the 60’s into the 70’s. The Railway Express Agency became the REA Express in it’s later years and the company disappeared altogether in the mid-1970’s.

Our model can be built in four different cowl style suggestive of the 30’s, late 40’s and 50’s. We plan to add some additional cowl styles soon. Each finished model is hand-build to order in our Colorado based studio, not China or some other far-off land. The $95.00 + shipping is a real bargain considering the time and effort that goes into each model. Anyone who has built one of our kits can tell you that while not a difficult kit to build, it does require time, patience and skill.

This is one of the most recent builds- it incorporates some of the details we have learned of as we find more information on these vehicles. The paint is particularly of note; the front cowl was painted black instead of green which appears to have been standard practice, based on photos we have seen. Our model represents a 1 ½ ton truck. We are in the process of mastering a ¾ ton version. It was shorter in length and did not use dual rear wheels, but was nearly identical in most other respects.

This model represents a common style 1930’s era truck although these old vehicles were used well into the 50’s and in some cases even later. This particular model was built for a customer who wanted an entire fleet! In addition to this truck we also built one of each of our available trucks for this customer. It proved to be quite an exercise in patience and made us aware of some needed changes and upgrade to the original kit.
